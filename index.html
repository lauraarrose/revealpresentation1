The Dismissal Project

Concept

Surreal game of Tinder in which a player swipes a moving image of a face left to reject and right to accept, and then must watch the consequences of their decision

What Happens When you Swipe

Left

Video changes and the face begins to dissolve into nothing while also looking disappointed and upset, subtly. Maybe the color in the room will change - darker and cooler - and maybe there will be some sound.

Right

Video changes and the face begins to glow and possibly expand while also looking satisfied and proud, subtly. Maybe the color of the room will change - brighter and warmer - and maybe there will be some sound.

Who Am I Filming?

The people must be diverse in age and appearance, and with neutral expressions to begin with - blinking at you curiously.

I will need a large sample of people so that swiping can be repeated and the player won't get the same face twice.

AND THEN WHAT?

The objective is to force people to look at each other and see each other as human beings before and after they dismiss them/make snap judgments.

If it's done well I'm hoping people will feel very uncomfortable and MAYBE EVEN CRY.

And maybe laugh. Part of me wants some of the faces to do funny things (or if not funny, exaggerated - like get angry). Most of me wants the reactions to be very subtle.

Problems I'm Expecting and WORRIED About

How to get my faces to express genuine emotion!

I've never really filmed anyone before - how do I make sure everyone is in the same spot and not moving around but also STAYING GENUINE (Requiring MULTITASKING from the participants worries me a little).

Manipulating the videos with code or with some other program first? Do I have time to do this well if I currently know very little about most things???
